Pizza

Pizza is a savory dish of Italian origin consisting of a usually round, flattened base of leavened wheat-based dough topped with tomatoes, cheese, and often various other ingredients (such as anchovies, mushrooms, onions, olives, pineapple, meat, etc.), which is then baked at a high temperature, traditionally in a wood-fired oven. A small pizza is sometimes called a pizzetta.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 3 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup cornmeal
  • 1/2 cup pizza sauce
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup sliced pepperoni, or as desired
  • 1 cup sliced fresh mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup chopped green bell pepper
  • 1/4 cup chopped onion
  • 1/4 cup sliced black olives

Directions

  1. Combine water, sugar, and yeast in a small bowl. Let stand until creamy, about 10 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour, olive oil, salt, and the yeast mixture. Using a stand mixer with a dough hook attachment, knead until a smooth, elastic dough forms, about 8 minutes. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour.
  3. Place dough in a well-oiled bowl and turn to coat. Cover with a damp cloth and let rise in a warm place until doubled in volume, about 1 hour.
  4. Preheat oven to 450 degrees F (230 degrees C). Punch down dough and turn it out onto a lightly floured surface. Use a rolling pin to roll the dough into a round shape. Transfer to a pizza pan sprinkled with cornmeal.
  5. Spoon pizza sauce over dough and top with mozzarella cheese. Add desired toppings and sprinkle with Parmesan cheese.
  6. Bake in preheated oven until cheese is bubbly and golden, about 15 minutes.
